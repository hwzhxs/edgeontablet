Agentic AI vs Generative AI: Understanding the Key Differences

MyScale · Jun 8, 2024 · 12 min read

Artificial Intelligence (AI) has evolved significantly over the past few decades, leading to the development of various specialized forms. Two of the most discussed types in recent times are Agentic AI and Generative AI. While both fall under the broad umbrella of AI, they serve different purposes and operate in distinct ways.

What is Agentic AI?

Agentic AI refers to AI systems that can make decisions and take actions autonomously to achieve specific goals. Unlike traditional AI, which follows predefined rules, Agentic AI is proactive — it can adapt to changing situations and handle complex tasks that require ongoing problem-solving and decision-making.

What is Generative AI?

Generative AI focuses on creating new content, such as text, images, music, or video. It is reactive, responding to input based on learned patterns, but it cannot make its own decisions or adapt to new situations independently.

Key Differences

AspectAgentic AIGenerative AI
AutonomyHigh — makes independent decisionsLow — responds to prompts
BehaviorGoal-directed, proactiveTask-oriented, reactive
AdaptationReal-time learningNo real-time adaptation
Decision MakingComplex, multi-stepBasic, pattern-based
